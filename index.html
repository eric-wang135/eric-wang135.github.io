Research Overview

My research focuses on developing deep learning methods for biomedical signal processing, especially for surface electromyography and electrocardiography. I am interested in reliable physiological signal enhancement, signal quality assessment, super-resolution, and multimodal learning under real-world noise and data-quality challenges.

Featured Research

Surface EMG Enhancement
Surface EMG Enhancement and Denoising
Developed deep learning methods for improving contaminated surface EMG signals under realistic noise conditions. This research explores multiple architectures and learning paradigms, including representation-masking Transformers, score-based diffusion models, and efficient Mamba-based networks, aiming to improve the reliability of surface EMG signals for downstream analysis.
[TrustEMG-Net] [SDEMG] [MSEMG] [SDEMG Code] [MSEMG Code]
Signal Quality Assessment
Surface EMG Signal Quality Assessment
Developed neural quality assessment methods for surface EMG signals, aiming to estimate signal usability without requiring clean reference signals. This research supports more reliable physiological signal analysis in clinical and wearable applications.
[arXiv] [PubMed]
Physiological Signal Super-Resolution
ECG and sEMG Super-Resolution
Explored efficient neural architectures for reconstructing high-resolution physiological signals from low-resolution measurements, with applications to ECG and surface EMG signal acquisition.
[MSECG Paper] [arXiv]
Speech Enhancement and Multimodal Learning
Robust Speech Enhancement and Acoustic/EMG Multimodal Learning
Worked on robust speech enhancement and multimodal speech processing, including acoustic/EMG fusion and the integration of pre-trained speech enhancement and recognition models.
